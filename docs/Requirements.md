# NexHack 2026

## 1. Introduction

the hackathon challenges participants to build AI systems for real enterprise operations and fintech trust problems. Open to university students and working adults in Malaysia; cross-functional teams combining technical ability, product thinking, and business judgment are encouraged.

---

## Hackathon Philosophy: Build What the Market Will Pay For

Winning solutions must solve painful problems that real organizations, fintech companies, or customers would **realistically pay to adopt**.

| Pillar | What It Means |
|---|---|
| **Technical depth** | Real system design, sound architecture, rational AI use, good UX |
| **Business depth** | Target customers, pain point, pricing models, roadmap, commercialization potential |
| **Depth beats breadth** | One practical problem solved deeply > many shallow features |
| **Builder mindset** | Think like product builders and entrepreneurs, not only coders |

---

## Problem Statement

**Building Autonomous AI Workforce & Trusted Fintech Ecosystems**

Organizations face pressure to improve productivity, reduce operational risk, and respond faster to fraud, compliance, and customer threats. Participants must design and implement an **AI-powered system** that can assist, automate, or intelligently coordinate real business workflows while remaining explainable, controllable, and realistic for adoption.

> Depth of execution matters more than topic choice. Judges reward teams that solve one practical problem deeply.

### Tracks

| Track | Domain |
|---|---|
| **Track 1** | Agentic AI for Internal Enterprise Operations *(HR, Finance, Compliance, IT Operations, etc.)* |
| **Track 2** | Fintech Risk & Fraud Intelligence *(Fraud Detection, AML, Credit Risk, Scam Prevention, etc.)* |

---

## Rubric

| Criteria | Weight | What Judges Look For | Full-Mark Indicators |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Problem Relevance & Impact** | 20 marks | Solves a real business or fintech problem; significant pain point | Clear target user, real workflow pain, measurable operational or risk impact |
| **Technical Architecture, Execution & Product Completeness** | 30 marks | Quality of architecture & implementation, working prototype, AI workflow, agent orchestration, reliability, completeness | End-to-end flow works; meaningful AI logic; good data handling; clear architecture diagram; polished demo |
| **Market Adoption & Commercial Potential** | 30 marks | Realistic adoption path; clear target market and value proposition | Defined customers, adoption path, implementation roadmap, compliance considerations |
| **Innovation & Differentiation** | 30 marks | Unique approach vs. existing solutions; novel use of AI or automation | Not just a chatbot — uses agents, workflow automation, explainability, proactive alerts, or domain-specific intelligence |
| **Presentation & Demonstration** | 20 marks | Clarity of pitch, demo quality, storytelling, ability to communicate value | Strong story, clear demo, concise slides, confident answers, convincing business value |

**Total: 130 marks**
